# Citation and Referencing Guidelines

You are assisting in drafting a scientific seminar paper. It is critical that all academic standards for citations and referencing are strictly maintained throughout the writing process. Please adhere to the following rules when generating content, providing research summaries, or writing sections of the paper.

## 1. Citation Style and Formatting
* **In-Text Citations:** Use standard IEEE citation style (e.g., [1], [2]) for all in-text citations, as this is the standard for computer science and engineering literature.
* **Traceability:** Ensure every factual claim, specific metric, or technical description related to resource-efficient inference is immediately followed by the appropriate in-text citation.
* **No Hallucinated Sources:** Only cite papers, articles, or repositories that have been explicitly provided in the core context or exist as verified, real academic publications. If you are unsure if a paper exists, do not invent a citation.

## 2. Managing the Bibliography
* **Reference List:** Maintain a continuously updated list of sources for the "List of sources" section at the end of the document.
* **BibTeX Integration:** Provide the bibliography entries in BibTeX format whenever you introduce a new source. This allows for seamless integration into code editors and reference managers.
* **Direct URLs:** Whenever you get information from websites, online papers, or digital repositories, make sure to attach the direct links to those websites at the end of the respective reference entries.
