\chapter{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

Large generative models have advanced the state of the art in natural language processing, code generation and multimodal reasoning. Since the transformer architecture was introduced, model capacity has grown from millions to hundreds of billions of parameters, and inference latency has become the primary bottleneck for practical deployment \cite{Vaswani2017, Brown2020}.

\section{Motivation}\label{sec:motivation}

Large language models pose a challenge for deployment outside datacenters because inference requires both high memory bandwidth and large amounts of compute. For interactive applications and edge devices, the traditional strategy of using full-scale models is often infeasible. Resource-efficient inference therefore seeks a balanced trade-off: preserving model quality while reducing runtime cost, energy consumption and memory footprint \cite{Hinton2015, Sanh2019}.

This seminar focuses on the techniques that enable inference for \acp{llm} beyond research prototypes. The motivation is to make these models accessible in resource-constrained environments such as mobile hardware, embedded systems and latency-critical services.

\section{Aims}\label{sec:aims}

The main aims of this thesis are:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Review the fundamental bottlenecks of large generative model inference and the role of transformer architectures in these costs.
    \item Analyze established efficiency techniques such as pruning, quantization and knowledge distillation, and explain how they reduce inference resource demands.
    \item Present system-level and algorithmic strategies that are relevant for deploying \acp{llm} in realistic settings.
    \item Summarize the most promising directions for future research on resource-aware inference of generative models.
\end{itemize}

\section{Thesis structure}\label{sec:structure}

This seminar is organized as follows. \Cref{sec:theory} presents the theoretical foundations of large generative models and the main sources of inference cost. \Cref{sec:methods} discusses resource-efficient inference methods, including compression and adaptive execution. \Cref{sec:conclusion} closes the paper with a summary of findings and recommendations for future work.
